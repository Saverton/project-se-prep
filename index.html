Biography

Hello! I am an up and coming software engineer who loves coding. in September of 2021, I found my passion for coding through taking the AP Computer Science course at my high school. I love learning new concepts and languages, and I also love working on personal projects to improve my skills.

I made it a personal goal to complete every last practice problem in our computer science textbook by the time of the AP test so that I would have a solid foundation in the fundamentals of programming. Furthermore, I sought out online resources to expand my knowledge and capabilities, enrolling in Harvard's GD50 course, all about the fundamentals of game development. Using what I learned from this course, I have worked on a small handful of larger video game projects for fun.

Having graduated from High School, I am now seeking an entryway into a career of software development, and have decided to go about it in a less traditional way. Rather than go straight into college, I am enrolling in a coding boot camp known as the Flatiron School. Once I have graduated from their incredible Software Engineeringprogram, I will seek an entry-level developer position, and gain some experience in the field before pouring excessive time and money into a degree.

Space Invaders

This is my first full game project using Lua and the LOVE2D framework! This began as a practice project, testing out my abilities within the new language and framework, but soon developed into a full on project. Once my version of the original game was established (the player, the ships, the shield elements, etc.), I started building in polish features so that the game could be shared among friends and family. I experimented with creating my own GUI classes, difficulty variation, animations, sound design, high-score saving and even a super-secret boss battle on level 12! The game is fun and addicting (not to mention tough!), and it is almost as fun to play as it was to code!

Asteroids

Asteroids was another project created with a similar purpose to Space invaders, although it didn't receive quite as much attention from me beyond getting the base gameplay to work properly. This creation was also a bit more faithful to the original arcade style Asteroids game when compared to Space Invaders and its inspiration. Random RPG (WIP)

Random RPG is my latest (and currently occupying) project, a top-down action RPG game not dissimilar from the classic Legend of Zelda titles on the NES! The original LoZ (1986) was one of my favorite old games to go back and play. It seemed very large and complex for its time, which it was able to accomplish through some genious shortcuts to fit all of its content on the very limited cartridges of the time. My game, hence the name, involves some randomness as well. This is a big part of the game that is still work in progress, but essentially the worlds you will adventure in will be randomized in a variety of different ways. This is already implemented, but not to the extend to which I hope to achieve one day! For more detailed information and more visuals, visit the project's linked GitHub and YouTube development playlist.
